How to size outdoor string lights the right way

The number one reason people run out of lights mid-project is forgetting sag. String lights are meant to droop into a gentle swag between poles, so the cable you actually hang is longer than the straight-line distance. A 10–20% sag allowance covers most café-light looks; deeper, more dramatic swags can need 25% or more. This calculator adds your chosen percentage automatically, then rounds up to whole strands of the length you plan to buy so you never come up short.

Quick reference: how many strands?

A common question is "how many strands for a 20×20 ft deck?" For a perimeter run that is roughly 80 ft of edge; with 15% sag that's about 92 ft, or four 25 ft strands. A single crisscross from corner to corner across the same deck is two diagonals of ~28 ft each, ~65 ft with sag, so three 25 ft strands. Always confirm your bulbs' max connectable strands before linking many end-to-end.

Set your pole spacing so lights stay taut enough to look intentional — 8–12 ft between posts is typical for patio and bistro lights. Closer spacing means a tighter line and less sag; wider spacing looks relaxed but needs guy wire support. The diagram above redraws live so you can sanity-check the run before you order.

FAQ

How do I account for sag in string lights?

Add a sag allowance percentage to the straight-line distance. 10–15% gives a subtle swag, 20–25% gives a deeper, more decorative droop. This tool adds it for you and rounds up to whole strands.

Zigzag vs straight — which uses more lights?

A crisscross or zigzag canopy covers an area with several diagonal passes, so it almost always uses more total feet than a single perimeter or straight run. Use the layout buttons above to compare both for your exact dimensions.

How far apart should I space poles or anchor points?

8–12 ft (about 2.5–3.5 m) is the sweet spot for most café and patio string lights. Wider spans sag more and may need a steel guide/guy wire to carry the weight so the bulb cord isn't strained.

How many strands do I need for a 20×20 ft deck?

About four 25 ft strands for a perimeter run (≈92 ft with sag) or three for a single crisscross. Enter 20 × 20 above to see your exact count, bulbs and cost.
