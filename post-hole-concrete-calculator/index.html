How many bags of concrete per post hole?

Setting a post is simple math: the concrete fills the hole minus the space the post itself takes up. The volume of a round hole is π × (radius)² × depth. This calculator does that for you, subtracts the post's cross-section, totals it across every post, then rounds up to whole bags because you can't buy a partial bag. For most 4×4 fence posts in a 10 in × 30 in hole, you'll use roughly two 50 lb bags of fast-setting concrete per post.

A few field tips: dig 1/3 of the post's above-ground height below grade (a 6 ft fence post wants about 2 ft deep), add 4–6 in of gravel at the bottom for drainage so the post doesn't rot, and slope the concrete crown away from the post to shed water. Always buy one extra bag — holes are never perfectly cylindrical, and running short mid-pour means a weak cold joint.

FAQ

How many bags of concrete do I need for one fence post?

For a typical 10-inch-wide, 30-inch-deep hole with a 4×4 post, you'll need about 1–2 bags of 50 lb fast-setting concrete, or one 80 lb bag. Enter your exact hole size above for a precise count.

How deep should a fence post hole be?

Bury about 1/3 of the post's total length, or to below your local frost line — whichever is deeper. A 6 ft tall fence usually means a 2 ft (24 in) deep hole, plus a few inches of gravel.

Do I need to mix the concrete first?

For fence posts, fast-setting concrete can be poured dry into the hole, then water added on top — no mixing tub needed. For structural deck footings, mix to spec and consult local building codes.

How many 80 lb bags are in a cubic yard?

An 80 lb bag yields about 0.6 cu ft set, so it takes roughly 45 bags to make one cubic yard (27 cu ft). The calculator shows your total in both bags and cubic yards.
