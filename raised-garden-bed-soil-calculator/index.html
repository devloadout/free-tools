A standard raised bed is 10–12 in deep. You can fill the bottom third with logs, branches or coarse mulch (hügelkultur) to cut soil cost on deep beds.

How much soil for a raised garden bed?

To find the soil volume for a raised bed, multiply length × width × depth. For example, a classic 4 ft × 8 ft bed filled 10 inches deep needs about 26.7 cubic feet of soil — that's roughly 27 bags at 1 cu ft, 18 bags at 1.5 cu ft, or 14 bags at 2 cu ft. A 4×4 bed at the same depth needs about half that.

Always buy a little extra: bagged soil is sold by volume before it settles and compresses, and fresh beds slump several inches in the first season. The "add 10% for settling" option above builds that buffer in for you. If your total climbs above roughly 1 cubic yard (about 27 cu ft, or 18+ bags), compare prices on bulk soil delivery — it's usually far cheaper per cubic foot than bags once you hit that threshold.

For the best results, don't use bagged "topsoil" alone. A proven raised-bed blend is Mel's Mix: equal parts compost, coconut coir (or peat) and coarse vermiculite. It drains well, holds moisture and needs no native soil underneath.

Frequently asked questions

How many bags of soil for a 4x8 raised bed?

A 4 ft × 8 ft bed filled to 10 inches needs about 26.7 cu ft of soil: around 27 bags of 1 cu ft, 18 bags of 1.5 cu ft, or 14 bags of 2 cu ft. At a full 12-inch depth it's 32 cu ft, so add roughly 20% more bags.

How much soil for a 4x4 raised bed?

A 4 ft × 4 ft bed at 10 inches deep needs about 13.3 cu ft — roughly 9 bags of 1.5 cu ft. At 6 inches deep (for greens and herbs) it's only 8 cu ft.

How many cubic feet are in a cubic yard of soil?

There are 27 cubic feet in 1 cubic yard. Once your project needs more than about a cubic yard, bulk delivery is usually cheaper than buying bags.

Should I fill a raised bed to the very top?

Leave about a 1–2 inch lip below the rim so soil and mulch don't wash over the edge when you water. The "leave a 10% lip" option above accounts for this.

Can I use cheaper fill at the bottom of a deep bed?

Yes. For beds deeper than about 12 inches, fill the bottom third with logs, sticks, leaves or coarse compost (hügelkultur) and reserve quality soil for the top 8–10 inches where roots grow.
