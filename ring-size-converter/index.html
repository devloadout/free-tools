How to use this ring size converter

Online jewelry shops list sizes in different national standards, which makes ordering — especially a gift or an international purchase — easy to get wrong. This tool removes the guesswork: type a single number in whichever system you know and read off the equivalent everywhere else. The conversions are based on the published relationship between a ring’s inside diameter, its inside circumference (diameter × π), and the standard size scales used in the US/Canada, UK/Australia, the EU/ISO 8653 system, and Japan.

If your measurement falls between two listed sizes, the converter snaps to the nearest standard size and tells you when you are between sizes. For comfort-fit or wide bands, rings sit tighter, so most jewelers recommend choosing a quarter to a half size larger than your bare measurement.

Frequently asked questions

What is the most accurate way to find my ring size?

Measure the inside diameter of a ring that already fits the right finger and enter it above, or measure the finger’s circumference with a thin strip of paper. Diameter is the most precise input because it does not depend on how tightly the strip is pulled.

Is a US 7 the same as a UK 7?

No. The US and UK scales are completely different — a US 7 is roughly a UK N½. Always convert rather than copying the number across, which is exactly what this tool does for you.

What does the EU / ISO number mean?

The EU/ISO size is simply the inside circumference of the ring in millimetres, so an EU 54 ring measures about 54 mm around the inside.

Should I size up for a wide band?

Usually yes. Bands wider than about 6 mm feel snugger, so going up a quarter to a half size gives a comfortable fit.
