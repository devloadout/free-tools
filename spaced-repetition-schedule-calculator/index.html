How the spaced repetition schedule calculator works

Spaced repetition fights the forgetting curve by reviewing material at expanding intervals instead of cramming. The classic 1-3-7-14-30 Leitner schedule means you review one day after studying, then three days, then a week, two weeks, and a month later. Each successful recall stretches the next gap, so a topic moves from short-term to long-term memory with the fewest possible reviews. This tool turns that abstract pattern into real calendar dates you can actually act on.

Type the date you learned something (it defaults to today), choose a preset or enter your own comma-separated offsets, and every review date is computed instantly. Add multiple items to track several decks, chapters, or vocab sets in parallel. When the schedule looks right, download a .ics file with a built-in reminder alarm and import it into Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook — or copy the dates as plain text. Everything runs locally; nothing you enter is uploaded or stored.

Frequently asked questions

Which interval should I use? For exams in 1-2 months, 1-3-7-14-30 is ideal. For permanent knowledge (a language), add 60 and 120. SM-2 style (1-6-15-35) mirrors Anki's early curve.

Will the reminders work on my phone? Yes — the .ics includes a VALARM, so most calendar apps notify you at your chosen time on each review day.

Is my data private? Completely. All date math and .ics creation happen in your browser. No account, no tracking of your study topics.
