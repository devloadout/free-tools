How the tattoo healing timeline works

A new tattoo is an open wound, and healing happens in predictable stages. This calculator takes your tattoo date and maps those stages onto real calendar dates, then adjusts them based on your tattoo's size and placement — bigger, heavily-shaded pieces and high-friction spots like hands, feet and joints simply take longer to close up and re-skin. The result is a clear answer to the questions people anxiously search the day after getting inked: when can I swim, when can I work out, and when can I shower normally?

In the first 2–3 days your tattoo oozes plasma and ink and stays swollen, so you keep it clean and avoid soaking it. Around days 3–6 the surface tightens and starts to flake and itch — this is normal, and picking or scratching is what causes scabbing and ink loss. By weeks 2–3 the outer skin looks healed, which is when light exercise and gentle sweating become reasonable. The deeper layers, however, keep rebuilding for up to a month or more, which is why swimming, long soaks, and direct sun stay off-limits longest: submerging or burning the area before it has fully closed is the fastest way to fade your ink or pick up an infection.

Frequently asked questions

When can I swim after a new tattoo?

Most artists say no pools, lakes, oceans, or hot tubs until the tattoo is fully healed — typically around 3 to 4 weeks, and longer for large pieces. Submerging an unhealed tattoo risks infection and ink loss. The calculator gives you the exact date based on your tattoo.

When can I work out or go to the gym?

Light, low-sweat movement is usually fine after a few days, but heavy sweating and equipment friction should wait roughly 1–2 weeks. Avoid exercises that stretch or rub the tattooed area until the surface has finished peeling.

When can I take a long bath or shower?

Short, lukewarm showers are fine almost immediately — just don't soak the tattoo or blast it directly. Long baths, soaking, and hot tubs should wait until the tattoo is fully healed, the same window as swimming.

When can I expose my tattoo to the sun?

Keep a healing tattoo out of direct sun while it's peeling, since UV damages new ink and can burn the raw skin. Once healed, always use SPF 30+ on the tattoo to keep it sharp and prevent fading.

Is this medical advice?

No. These are general aftercare estimates. Always follow your tattoo artist's specific instructions and consult a doctor if you see signs of infection.
